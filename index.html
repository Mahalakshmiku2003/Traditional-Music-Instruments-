Sitar

String
Region: North India
Material: Teak wood, gourd, steel strings
Playing Style: Plucked with a plectrum (Mizrab)
The Sitar is synonymous with Indian classical music worldwide. It features a long neck and a gourd resonator, known for its intricate 'meend' (gliding notes) and 'jhala' (fast rhythmic patterns). Historically, it was refined in the Mughal courts and popularized globally by Pandit Ravi Shankar.
